Dance

I have been dancing ever since I was in Kindergarten. My mom started me on my dance journey at the local legacy center where I took a class with my childhood friend. I continued to dance in a studio, until I quit in the fifth grade. I did not like the teacher. Although I "quit" dance, I never quit dancing. It was during this studio dance hiatus that I found a love for all things hip-hop. I began teaching myself the urban-dance style. Now I can dance pretty hip for a small, 5'3, white girl from Lehi Utah. One of my favorite things is showing up to dance parties with people who do not know how well I dance and just absolutely knocking their socks off.

I figured you would probably like to see an infographic on the most common dance injuries based on dance stlye so I included it below. I will say that I have twisted a lot of ankles, and have given myself knee and wrist problems through dance.

Music

No dancer is complete without her music. Over-time I have become quite enamoured by Music. I fill empty space with music as much as possible. I have three categories of music that I typically listen to. I have my car music, my dancing music, and my studying music. I study to lo-fi beats. I enjoy studying to lo-fi, because there are no words and the music is just boring enough to not be distracting. My dance music would be anything with a strong beat. I love dancing to old-time hip-hop tracks. An example of my car music can be found below. I love indi or alternative-pop music. I hope you enjoy my song of the week: Whatever We Feel by Sammy Rae and the Friends.
